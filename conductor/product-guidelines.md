# Product Guidelines: Jattac.Libs.Web.ResponsiveTable

These guidelines define the standards for communication, interaction, and visual design to ensure a consistent and delightful experience for both developers and end-users.

## 1. Communication & Prose Style
- **Tone:** **Clear & Professional.** Focus on precision and clarity while maintaining a professional demeanor.
- **Developer Experience (DX):** Prioritize **Delightful DX**. 
    - **Minimal Boilerplate:** Components and plugins should be easy to set up with minimal ceremony.
    - **Sensible Defaults:** Provide robust default configurations so it "just works" out of the box.
    - **Convention over Configuration:** Follow established patterns to reduce cognitive load.
    - **Deep Documentation:** Maintain comprehensive guides and exhaustive examples for all features.

## 2. Interaction Principles
- **Core Priorities:**
    - **Speed & Efficiency:** Interactions must be instantaneous, supporting high-throughput user workflows.
    - **Feedback & Delight:** Utilize micro-interactions and visual feedback to create a sense of joy and responsiveness.
    - **Accessibility & Stability:** Maintain rigorous adherence to accessibility standards (ARIA) and ensure system stability across all use cases.

## 3. Visual & Aesthetic Standards
- **Modern Typography:** Prioritize high-quality, legible fonts and clean, hierarchy-driven layouts.
- **Smooth Animations:** Use purposeful transitions and motion to guide the user's focus and soften UI state changes.
- **Consistent Spacing:** Adhere to strict grid systems and maintain balanced negative space to ensure a "Quiet Luxury" yet "Corporate Playful" feel.

## 4. Error Handling & State Management
- **Error UX:** **Direct & Corrective.** When an error occurs or a state is empty, tell the user exactly what is happening and provide clear, actionable steps for correction.
- **Empty States:** Design empty states to be helpful, guiding the user toward the next step in their workflow.
